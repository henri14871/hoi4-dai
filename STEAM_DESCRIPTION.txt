[h1]DAI - Smarter AI[/h1]

[b]Why does Germany suicide its entire army into the Maginot?[/b]

You know the problem. It's 1940, Germany has 200 divisions and medium tanks, and it throws them all at the Alps and the Maginot Line instead of pushing through the Low Countries. The Soviet Union builds nothing but infantry and never upgrades. Japan forgets to garrison its conquests. Italy runs out of equipment in 1941 and just... stops.

This mod fixes that.

[h2]The Short Version[/h2]
DAI gives every AI nation a brain. Not a script — a [b]dynamic evaluation system[/b] that reads the board and reacts. Every country gets scored on industry, military, technology, and strategic position. That score drives every decision: what to research, what to build, when to attack, when to hold, and when to stop throwing men into a losing offensive.

[b]No hardcoded country logic.[/b] No "Germany does X in 1939." No nation-specific scripts. Every AI reads the same game state and makes decisions appropriate to its power, ideology, and situation. Works in vanilla. Works in Kaiserreich. Works in TNO. Works in BlackICE.

[h2]What Changes In Your Game[/h2]
[list]
[*] [b]AI actually builds supply infrastructure.[/b] Conquered territory gets railways, supply hubs, and infrastructure. No more grinding offensives to a halt because the AI forgot about logistics.
[*] [b]Garrisons scale with occupation.[/b] Germany garrisoning France properly. Japan holding its Pacific conquests. No more free resistance spirals.
[*] [b]AI stops ignoring its own crises.[/b] Equipment running low? The AI halts offensives, shifts production, and conscripts more manpower. No more suicidal attacks with empty stockpiles.
[*] [b]Smart posture shifts.[/b] The AI coordinates production, research, and warfare into unified strategic stances. Buildup mode. Defensive mode. Full offensive. Total war. Survival. All four modules pulling in the same direction.
[*] [b]Wars are decided by planning, not AI stupidity.[/b] If you lose, it's because the enemy outproduced and outmaneuvered you — not because the AI got lucky while the other side forgot to build tanks.
[*] [b]Late-game stays dangerous.[/b] 1944 AI is not the same as 1936 AI. Nations that industrialized are terrifyingly efficient. Nations that lost ground fight harder, not dumber.
[/list]

[h2]How Does It Work?[/h2]
Every country gets a [b]Global Power Score[/b] from 0-100, recalculated monthly. Industry counts for 35%, military strength for 30%, technology for 20%, and strategic position for 15%. That score maps to a competence scalar — superpowers play with higher effective intelligence than minor nations.

On top of that, DAI tracks 22 country variables in real time: supply strain, equipment health, occupied territory, air and naval threat, manpower, enemy armour capability, and more. Every AI strategy factor reads from these variables. The result is AI that [i]adapts[/i] — not AI that follows a script.

[b]Four modules, one system:[/b]
[list]
[*] [b]Research[/b] — 7 tech branches dynamically weighted by game state. Industrial powers research tanks. Naval powers research ships. Nations at war rush doctrine.
[*] [b]Army[/b] — adaptive division templates that scale with industry. Intelligent production that shifts with needs. Supply infrastructure construction. Reactive anti-tank deployment when facing enemy armour.
[*] [b]Warfare[/b] — 9-tier frontline control from emergency halt to full offensive. Armour concentration on weak sectors. Force massing for breakthrough. Coordinated naval invasions gated by readiness.
[*] [b]Grand Strategy[/b] — dynamic diplomacy, threat-based mobilisation, posture-driven economy, strategic peace conferences.
[/list]

[h2]Compatibility[/h2]
DAI ships with built-in profiles for 4 mod environments. No patches needed, no sub-mods, no load order fiddling. Auto-detection at startup.

[list]
[*] Vanilla (default)
[*] Kaiserreich
[*] The New Order
[*] BlackICE
[/list]

Each profile maps DAI's abstract categories to that mod's specific technologies, equipment, and unit types. Just enable both mods and play.
