[h1]DAI - Smarter AI[/h1]

[b]The AI finally plays the game you're playing.[/b]

Germany bleeds itself dry on the Maginot for two years. Japan conquers half of Asia and garrisons none of it. Italy runs out of rifles and attacks anyway. Britain ignores D-Day prep on its doorstep. The Soviet Union feeds 300 divisions into a meat grinder it should have stopped feeding months ago.

This is what vanilla HOI4 AI does. Every single game.

DAI fixes all of it.

[h2]What Actually Changes[/h2]

[b]The AI evaluates 40+ strategic variables every cycle[/b] -- industry, military strength, equipment health, fuel reserves, air threat, naval threat, invasion risk, supply strain, casualties, stability, war support, resistance, manpower, and more. Then it makes decisions that actually make sense.

[list]
[*] [b]D-Day doesn't surprise anyone anymore.[/b] Coastal nations detect landing craft research, enemy naval buildup, and troop concentrations. They garrison their coasts before the invasion launches, not after.
[*] [b]Air superiority actually matters.[/b] The AI scores enemy air power from factory count, tech level, and bomber capability -- not a binary yes/no check. When factories start getting bombed, it triggers emergency fighter and AA production.
[*] [b]Fuel changes everything.[/b] Nations running low on fuel stop building tanks and motorized divisions. They build synthetic refineries instead. Armored offensives don't launch when the tanks can't move.
[*] [b]The AI knows when to stop attacking.[/b] Heavy casualties, low equipment, front stagnation, and fuel shortage all push the AI out of suicidal offensives and into holding positions that conserve strength.
[*] [b]Puppets act like puppets.[/b] Subject nations defend their master's borders, don't start independent wars, and don't send lend-lease they can't afford. Faction leaders coordinate allies and send support.
[*] [b]Landlocked nations stop building navies.[/b] Hungary no longer wastes factories on dockyards. Island nations massively prioritize their fleets.
[*] [b]Multi-front wars get real scaling.[/b] Fighting 5 enemies produces a fundamentally different army plan than fighting 1. Reserves, division targets, and front allocation all scale with actual enemy count.
[*] [b]Capitals get emergency defense.[/b] When enemy troops reach neighboring provinces, everything redirects to survival.
[*] [b]Civil wars lock down.[/b] No more sending lend-lease abroad or preparing foreign wars while your own country is split in half.
[*] [b]Peace conferences make sense.[/b] Democracies liberate. Aggressive powers annex. Nuclear states assert dominance. The AI doesn't randomly puppet Luxembourg while ignoring strategic territory.
[/list]

[h2]How It Works[/h2]

Every AI nation gets a [b]Global Power Score[/b] computed from industry, military, technology, and strategic pressure. That score feeds a competence scalar that makes major powers plan further ahead while minors still behave sensibly.

One shared [b]strategic posture[/b] system coordinates the entire AI:

[list]
[*] [b]Buildup[/b] -- peacetime economy growth
[*] [b]Defensive[/b] -- hold the line, build reserves
[*] [b]Balanced[/b] -- standard wartime operations
[*] [b]Offensive[/b] -- concentrate armor, push weak sectors
[*] [b]Total War[/b] -- maximum military production
[*] [b]Survival[/b] -- everything to the front, abandon garrisons
[/list]

When posture shifts, [i]everything[/i] shifts: production, construction, research, diplomacy, frontline behavior, and laws. No more subsystems fighting each other.
